About me

I’m a researcher working on quantum error correction and quantum algorithms at the Russian Quantum Center. My focus here is on taking practical steps to the realization of fault-tolerant quantum computations and making use of NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) devices for realization of various error-correction codes and algorithms. See descriptions of my publications and early independent research projects in the Research & Software section. A separate strand of my research interests is how mathematics can describe processes in macroeconomics and development economics.

I got my Bachelor's degree from Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, department of Applied Mathematics and Control. Having received extensive training in the fundamental fields of mathematics and physics, I decided to gain an understanding of the processes governing institutions from the levels of individuals to international organizations’ levels. I entered the program Master of Arts in Economics at New Economic School, a leading Russian university in the field of economics. After graduation I started to apply cutting edge research in statistics and machine learning for risk modeling, pricing, and demand analysis, until I discovered that I enjoy research a lot. You can find a brief description of my previous work projects in the Work Projects section.
